The Story of Culinary Craft

How We Conquered the Fire

Cooking is not just nutrition—it is the ultimate human alchemy. Take a stroll down the organic milestones that shaped vegan, vegetarian, and fire-tossed cuisines across historical epochs.

1.8 Million Years Ago

The Dawn of Spark

Homo erectus discovers fire cooking, tenderizing wild roots, plants, and seeds, which unlocks highly digestible calories and ignites brain evolution.

10,000 BCE

The Clay Pot Revolution

Ancient Mesopotamia invents fired clay pottery. This allows human tribes to boil grains, brew wild herbs, and create the world's very first vegetable stews.

2000 BCE

Ancient Ribbons & Spices

Indus Valley tribes grind ginger, garlic, and turmeric to simmer early curries, while ancient Chinese cooks stretch ground millet flour into the world's very first ribbon noodles.

500 BCE

Pythagoras & Vegetarianism

Ancient philosopher Pythagoras establishes the "Pythagorean Diet" in Greece, teaching respect for all living things and popularizing plant-based nourishment.

1796 CE

First Recipe Scrolls

Amelia Simmons publishes "American Cookery," introducing systematic measurements and written recipe structures, turning cooking from trial into culinary art.

1889 CE

The Royal Pizza Margherita

Naples chef Raffaele Esposito crafts a royal flatbread using tomatoes, mozzarella, and green basil leaves to honor Queen Margherita, initiating the global modern pizza craze.

1904 CE

The Bun & Patty Revolution

At the St. Louis World's Fair, the modern hamburger debuts, sandwiching hot seared patties inside split buns and paving the path for modern artisanal plant-based burgers.

Present Day

Liquid Glass Molecular Dining

Culinary fields embrace sensory chemistry and plant physics, crafting plant burgers and coconut custards that rival historical masterpieces in texture and taste.
